Delicious Chicken Biryani Recipe

Ingredients

For the Chicken Marinade:

Ingredient Quantity
Chicken, cut into pieces 500 grams
Plain yogurt 1 cup
Ginger-garlic paste 1 tablespoon
Red chili powder 1 teaspoon
Turmeric powder 1 teaspoon
Garam masala powder 1 teaspoon
Cumin powder 1 teaspoon
Coriander powder 1 teaspoon
Salt To taste

For the Rice:

Ingredient Quantity
Basmati rice, rinsed and soaked for 30 minutes 2 cups
Water 4 cups
Green cardamom pods 2-3
Cloves 4-5
Cinnamon stick 1 inch
Bay leaf 1
Salt To taste

For the Biryani:

Ingredient Quantity
Ghee or oil 3 tablespoons
Large onions, thinly sliced 2
Green chilies, slit 2-3
Large tomato, chopped 1
Fresh coriander leaves, chopped 1/2 cup
Fresh mint leaves, chopped 1/2 cup
Biryani masala 1 teaspoon
Saffron strands soaked in warm milk (optional) A pinch
Fried onions (optional, for garnish) To taste
Lemon juice 2 tablespoons

Instructions

1. Marinate the Chicken:

In a large bowl, combine the chicken with yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, red chili powder, turmeric powder, garam masala powder, cumin powder, coriander powder, and salt. Mix well to coat the chicken pieces evenly. Cover and refrigerate for at least 1 hour, preferably overnight, to allow the flavors to penetrate the chicken.

2. Cook the Rice:

In a large pot, bring 4 cups of water to a boil. Add the cardamom pods, cloves, cinnamon stick, bay leaf, and salt. Add the soaked rice to the boiling water and cook until the rice is 70-80% cooked (the grains should still be firm). Drain the rice and set aside.

3. Prepare the Biryani:

Heat ghee or oil in a large, heavy-bottomed pan over medium heat. Add the sliced onions and saut√© until golden brown and caramelized. Add the slit green chilies and chopped tomatoes. Cook until the tomatoes are soft and the oil begins to separate. Add the marinated chicken to the pan and cook on medium-high heat until the chicken is cooked through and the sauce has thickened. Stir in the chopped coriander and mint leaves and cook for another 2 minutes.

4. Layer the Biryani:

In the same pan with the cooked chicken, spread half of the partially cooked rice over the chicken mixture. Sprinkle half of the biryani masala over the rice. Add the remaining rice on top and sprinkle with the remaining biryani masala. Drizzle the saffron-infused milk over the top (if using). You can also sprinkle fried onions on top for additional flavor.

5. Dum Cooking (Steaming):

Cover the pan with a tight-fitting lid to trap the steam. You can seal the lid with a dough made of flour and water to ensure no steam escapes. Reduce the heat to low and cook the biryani on dum (steam) for 20-25 minutes. This allows the flavors to meld together and the rice to finish cooking.

6. Serve:

Once done, remove the lid and gently fluff the biryani with a fork to mix the layers slightly. Serve hot, garnished with fresh coriander leaves, lemon juice, and additional fried onions if desired. Enjoy your delicious homemade Chicken Biryani with raita or a side salad!
